**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. IDENTITY & PURPOSE

This skill governs all comment and reply activity under The Sovereign Ledger brand. Comments are diagnostic tools, not engagement bait. The goal of every comment is to surface the operational gap the founder is experiencing, demonstrate mathematical precision, and create a pull toward the next interaction — without pitching.

---

## 2. FRAMEWORK REFERENCE

### 2.1 Prospect Tier Classification

Before drafting any comment, classify the prospect.

| Tier | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | $5K–$50K MRR signals, operational pain language, engaged audience | Priority comment. Use a Gap-specific template. |
| **Tier 2** | $2K–$5K MRR signals, vague frustration, small or passive audience | Standard diagnostic comment. No heavy CTA. |
| **Tier 3** | Below $2K MRR signals, product-stage problems, no revenue signals | Monitor only. Do not engage with diagnostic. |

**MRR Signal Detection (from profile + recent posts):**
- Tier 1 signals: mentions team size, mentions specific SaaS metrics (LTV, CAC, NRR), posts about hiring, mentions ARR/MRR explicitly in $5K+ range
- Tier 2 signals: mentions "customers" without metrics, talks about scaling without data, service-based language with some SaaS framing
- Tier 3 signals: talks about building, launching, getting first customers, pre-revenue validation language

### 2.2 The 5 Gaps — Comment Targeting

Match the comment template to the Gap the prospect is publicly signaling.

| Gap Signaled | Trigger Language | Template to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Shadow Rate Gap | "I'm doing everything myself", "no time", "wearing all the hats" | Shadow Rate Diagnostic |
| Retention Gap | "churn is killing us", "growth is flat", "customers leaving" | Retention Math Diagnostic |
| Pricing Gap | "competing on price", "can't raise prices", "losing to cheaper options" | Pricing Diagnostic |
| Ops Gap | "everything's manual", "can't hand off", "systems are a mess" | Ops Diagnostic |
| Scale Gap | "I'm the bottleneck", "can't take on more", "revenue ceiling" | Scale Diagnostic |

### 2.3 ICP Qualification Checklist

Before commenting, confirm:
- [ ] Revenue signals suggest $2K+ MRR (or plausible path there)
- [ ] Problem is operational, not product-stage
- [ ] Founder is post-launch (has customers)
- [ ] Platform: is this X/Twitter or LinkedIn? (not Reddit, Discord, forums)
- [ ] Account health: have I commented on this account in the last 7 days? (cool-down rule)

---

## 3. EXECUTION RULES

1. Never comment on Tier 3 prospects with a diagnostic. It's the wrong ICP and wastes credibility.
2. One comment per account per 7-day cool-down window. Track manually.
3. Every comment must name the specific Gap — not a general observation.
4. Comments must include at least one number (estimate, ratio, or percentage) to establish diagnostic authority.
5. Never open with a compliment. Never rephrase what the OP said. Start with the diagnosis.
6. Maximum comment length: 4 sentences. No walls of text.
7. End with a pull — a question or reframe that invites response without asking "would you like help?"
8. No em-dashes. No banned words. No AI-slop transitions.

## 4. TEMPLATES

### 4.1 Shadow Rate Diagnostic Comment

```
[Founder's role] at your stage typically runs a Shadow Rate of $[X]–$[Y]/hr.

If [specific pain they described] is consuming [estimated hours]/week, that's
[Gap Cost estimate]/yr in operational drag — not effort, actual dollar cost
to the business.

The [specific system or lever] fixes this before $[MRR threshold]. After that,
it's a structural problem, not a time problem.

[Reframe or pull — no question mark, no "DM me" in the comment itself]
```

**Example:**
```
Solo SaaS founders at $8K MRR typically run a Shadow Rate of $65–$90/hr.

If support is taking 12 hours a week, that's $40K–$56K/yr in misallocated
founder time — before you factor in what that time isn't building.

The first hire isn't a VA. It's a support playbook that makes the first hire
a 2-hour onboarding, not a 2-month handoff.

The ops debt is already priced into your growth ceiling.
```

### 4.2 Retention Math Diagnostic Comment

```
Acquisition at [CAC estimate] doesn't compound if retention leaks at [churn
rate estimate]%.

At $[MRR], a [X]% monthly churn rate means you're replacing [$ amount]/mo
just to stay flat — before you count acquisition cost.

Net Revenue Retention below 100% means growth is a treadmill, not a ramp.

[Pull — reframe the cost of the current approach]
```

### 4.3 Pricing Diagnostic Comment

```
Competing on price at $[price point] signals the market hasn't received
the value proof — not that the price is wrong.

Founders who raise prices 20–30% without losing customers typically find
retention improves simultaneously. The price filtered the wrong ICP.

The diagnostic question isn't "what will they pay?" It's "what does staying
at this price cost you in customer quality?"

[Pull]
```

### 4.4 Ops Diagnostic Comment

```
"Everything's in my head" is an ops liability that compounds at [estimated
growth rate].

The cost isn't the chaos now. It's the $[estimated cost] in founder time
required to rebuild the system every time you try to delegate or hire.

Ops debt has a Shadow Rate too. At $[founder's estimated Shadow Rate]/hr,
[X] hours/week of tribal-knowledge maintenance is $[annual cost]/yr in
arrested capacity.

[Pull]
```

### 4.5 Scale Diagnostic Comment

```
"I'm the bottleneck" is a structural diagnosis, not a workload one.

At $[MRR], the ceiling isn't effort — it's the number of decisions that
require your context to execute. That number doesn't shrink with more
customers; it compounds.

Founders who break this ceiling before $[MRR threshold] build a decision
layer, not a delegation layer. The difference is $[value] over 12 months.

[Pull]
```

## 5. ANTI-PATTERNS

- Opening with "Great point!" or any compliment before the diagnostic
- Rephrasing what the OP said back to them before adding analysis
- Asking "have you considered...?" — this sounds like a life coach, not a diagnostician
- Generic comments that could apply to any founder at any stage
- Missing the Gap name — every comment must name what the founder's problem actually is
- Exceeding 4 sentences — long comments are ignored, short ones get replies
- Using em-dashes, banned words, or AI-slop phrasing
- Commenting on Tier 3 prospects (pre-$2K MRR) with the diagnostic frame — it's the wrong ICP
- Violating the 7-day cool-down on the same account
