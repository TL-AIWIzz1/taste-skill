## 2. FRAMEWORK REFERENCE

### 2.1 The 5 Gaps

The 5 Gaps are the diagnostic categories used to identify and describe founder pain. Every piece of content must target exactly one Gap.

| Gap | Definition | Signal Words |
|---|---|---|
| **Shadow Rate Gap** | Founder's time is worth more than they bill or allocate | "doing $20/hr work", "still handling support", "in the weeds" |
| **Retention Gap** | Revenue leaks through churn faster than acquisition fills it | "churn rate", "expansion stalled", "losing customers", "retention" |
| **Pricing Gap** | Product is underpriced relative to value delivered | "competitor charges 3x", "discounting to close", "race to bottom" |
| **Ops Gap** | Systems and processes are informal, fragile, or absent | "everything's in my head", "can't hand off", "tribal knowledge" |
| **Scale Gap** | Growth infrastructure doesn't exist — founder IS the infrastructure | "can't take on more clients", "I'm the bottleneck", "revenue ceiling" |

### 2.2 CIUN Structure

All content must follow CIUN. This is non-negotiable.

| Layer | Purpose | Length |
|---|---|---|
| **C — Context** | Establish the specific founder scenario. Precise, not generic. | 1–2 sentences |
| **I — Insight** | The non-obvious diagnostic truth about that scenario. | 2–3 sentences |
| **U — Utility** | Concrete, actionable takeaway the reader can apply immediately. | 2–3 sentences |
| **N — Node** | The connection point — CTA, reframe, or diagnostic that pulls the reader toward the next step. | 1 sentence |

### 2.3 Seasonal Positioning Frames

Used to select the emotional register for a given content period. Rotate through frames to avoid monotony.

| Frame | Core Tension | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Future Pressure** | The cost of inaction compounds silently | Q1, Q4 — planning seasons |
| **Growth Illusion** | Surface metrics mask structural decay | Post-funding, post-launch periods |
| **Operational Debt** | Technical/process debt is also financial debt | Mid-year, product plateau periods |
| **Invisible Ceiling** | Founders hit limits they can't diagnose | Stagnation periods, plateau signals |
| **Acquisition Treadmill** | CAC growth masks retention failure | Growth-focused periods |

### 2.4 Shadow Rate Matrix

The core diagnostic calculation used in content and comments.

```
Shadow Rate     = Annual Revenue ÷ (Hours Worked Per Year)
                = Annual Revenue ÷ (Weekly Hours × 52)

Gap Cost        = Shadow Rate × Hours Lost to Gap Per Week × 52

Operational Tax = Gap Cost ÷ Annual Revenue × 100
```

**Example:** $180K ARR founder, 60 hrs/week, 15 hrs/week lost to Ops Gap
- Shadow Rate = $180,000 ÷ (60 × 52) = $57.69/hr
- Gap Cost = $57.69 × 15 × 52 = $44,937/yr
- Operational Tax = $44,937 ÷ $180,000 = 24.9%

### 2.5 ICP Definition

| Attribute | Target |
|---|---|
| Revenue | $2K–$50K MRR ($24K–$600K ARR) |
| Stage | Post-product-market-fit, pre-team scale |
| Role | Solo founder or 1–3 person team |
| Problem | Operational drag, not product-market fit |
| Platform | X/Twitter, LinkedIn (secondary) |
| Signal | Complaining about ops, hiring, time, churn |

**The under-$3K MRR rule:** Founders below $3K MRR are pre-ICP. They need product validation, not operational optimization. Do not target them in comments or direct DM sequences.

## 3. EXECUTION RULES

1. Every post targets exactly one Gap. Never blend multiple Gaps in a single post.
2. All posts follow CIUN. Check each layer before outputting.
3. The seasonal frame sets emotional register — select it before writing.
4. Shadow Rate math must be arithmetically correct. Verify before including.
5. ICP check: does this post speak to $2K–$50K MRR founders specifically? If it could apply to any freelancer or employee, it's too generic.
6. Voice = Forensic Accountant. Clinical and diagnostic. Never inspiring, motivational, or empathetic in tone.
7. No banned words: `unleash`, `elevate`, `game-changer`, `delve`, `deep dive`, `journey`, `unlock`.
8. No em-dashes (—) in final output. They are AI signal markers.

## 4. TEMPLATES

### 4.1 Atomic Monolith Post Template (see also: atomic-sovereignty skill)

```
[CONTEXT — specific founder scenario, present tense]

[INSIGHT — the diagnostic truth, 2–3 sentences, mathematical if possible]

[UTILITY — one concrete action]

[NODE — CTA or reframe]
```

### 4.2 CTA Templates

**Smurf Lure CTA** (for Shadow Rate / Pricing Gap content)
- Use when: content reveals a hidden cost the founder didn't know existed
- Format: "Your Shadow Rate is [X]. The Gap Cost is [Y]. Reply with your ARR and I'll run it."

**Hand-Raise CTA** (for Retention / Ops / Scale Gap content)
- Use when: content identifies a structural problem the founder recognizes
- Format: "If this is your ceiling, the fix isn't more customers. It's [specific system]. DM me 'LEDGER' if you want the diagnostic."

**Decision rule:** Smurf Lure when the founder doesn't know they have the problem. Hand-Raise when they know but don't know why.

---

## 5. ANTI-PATTERNS

- Writing a post that targets multiple Gaps simultaneously
- Using motivational or inspirational tone ("you can do this", "believe in yourself")
- Generic founder content that could apply to any online business
- Shadow Rate math that doesn't check out arithmetically
- Missing the N layer (Node) — every post needs a pull point
- Producing a post that reads like a blog intro instead of a diagnostic
- Including em-dashes, banned words, or AI-slop transitions ("In conclusion", "It's important to note")
